Timeline of my Career

Central Michigan University, Bachelor of Applied Arts

August 2015 - May 2019

I attended Central Michigan (CMU) from Fall 2015 to Spring 2019. Like most, 18-year-olds I went to college for the first time having no idea what I wanted to do. This is where my habbit for trying differnet career paths was born. I started as a journalism major, thinking
I wanted to write for IGN. Later, realizing I didn't want to be told what to write about for the rest of my life, switch to advertising, then while I liked parts of journalism and advertising, I didn't want my career to be so narrow doing one thing for the rest of my life. I finally declared my major in Integrative Public Relations (IPR). I chose IPR becuase it required me to take journalism, advertising, PR, marketing, business, and broadcast classes. I've always loved the idea of being well rounded and having experience in multiple professions. An IPR degree was the first step in my life-long journey to becoming a jack-of-all-trades.

Scott Fosgard Communications

May 2019 - August 2019

Scott Fosgard (SFC) was my first (and only) internship. I started at SFC right after my final semster at CMU and this internship counted toward my required internship credit for my Bachelor degree. SFC was a 100% remote agency (before remote was cool) based out of Detroit, Michigan. I'm thankful to Scott Fosgard (CEO & Founder) for giving me the opportunity to experience what it's like to be a true PR professional and pitching media. SFC focused on serving global clients in the autonomus vehicle industry (my first taste of startups) where we worked with Journalist from the New York Times, Forbes, and Bloomberge. However, the experience also taught me that agency life wasn't for me and I wanted my work to be for one company and it's projects only.

Domino's Pizza Inc.

November 2019 - October 2021

After finishing my internship at SFC, I recieved an offer to work as a Product Marketing & Communications Associate at Domino's corporate HQ in Ann Arbor, Michigan. This was my full-time job that required having a degree. This was my first taste of what it was like to work with engineers and started my love for working on and building software products that impact users daily lives. I joined the Next Generate Store Systems team (NGSS), which was tasked with building a new poiont-of-sales sytem from the ground up. Domino's original POS system was built on Visual Basic from a third-party company (that was no longer in business) and Microsoft announced it would no longer support Visual Basic. Being a part of an enterprise company like Domino's investing the time and resources to build a new product from the ground up was an experince I didn't realize was so valuable at the time. Working onsite during alpha test, talking with end-users along with creating training materials in confluence and product videos in the Adobe Suite made me fall in love with the process of building and refining software products.

Oakland University, Master of Business Administration

May 2021 - March 2024

Shortly after my 1-year-mark at Domino's, I decided to continue my educatoin and get my MBA. Seeing the complex in's and out's of what it was like working with various internal and external stakeholders, engineers, and product managers I felt obtaining an MBA would give me a mor well-rounded understanding of differnet business units. From May 2021 to December 2023 I worked on my MBA while working full-time taking between 2-3 classes per semester depending on my employment workload per quarter. My last semster in January 2024 I decided to join a full-time coding bootcamp and finish my final MBA semester at night.

Veryable

October 2021 - March 2022

With the SaaS industry exploding starting in 2018, I kept hearing the term SaaS Sales and it perked my interest. If you asked me when I was in college if I would ever take a sales job, my response would be a hard "no". Yet, once I join Veryable, I ended up have a 3-year tech sales career with many success stories and just as many failures; and I wouldn't change it for the world. Veryable is a gig-economy app for manufacturing business to leverage workes on a flexiable as-needed basis, similar to how Uber allows users to use drives on an as-needed basis. My time at Veryable was short, but impactful for my development. It gave me the sales, bug, I had this uncontrollable itch to prove I could be a top-performing sales rep, however, I knew it wouldn't be at Veryable. Veryable's business model was more about the concept than the software, and my interest was and has always been around the software. I wanted to sell impactful software products, and I found that the manufacturing industry wasn't for me. The biggest lesson I learned at Veryable is the idea that "You have to believe in what your selling", this idea is obsurdly wrong. To be a truly successful sales rep, beliveing in what yourseling is easy, you have to be excited to talk about what you're selling. I found that excitment at Codility.

Codility

March 2022 - February 2023

The highlight of my tech sales career. Codility is a technical assesment platform that helps hiring teams evaluate engineering applicants coding skills to hire the best talent faster (ironic that I ended up becoming a software engineeer, I know). Not a day goes by when I think back to my Codility days and not smile. I made life-long friends and mentors here that allowed me to solidify my selling and stakeholder managment skills. Starting on the inbound SDR team handling consoltatoin calls with customers, to being promoted to the outbound team (fastest in company history) and becoming the top performer, I learn that no one succeeds 100% on their own. I owe so much of my success to the friends and mentors I gained at Codility; their guidence and support, will carry on for the rest of my professional career.
